Personal licence course & delivery

The course covers a range of licensing areas including premises and personal licences, protection of children, enforcement and penalties, and responsibilities of alcohol retailing.
We offer three delivery methods, so you can choose whichever suits you or your team best:
  • Onsite delivery — we come to your venue and deliver the course to a group of up to 15 candidates
  • E-learning — study the course content in your own time via our e-learning module, then take the exam remotely
  • Online classroom — join a live online classroom session, then your remotely invigilated exam is arranged within 5 working days afterwards
Contact us to discuss which option is right for you.
The course is normally delivered in one day, 9am to 4.30pm, including the examination. For onsite courses the timings can be arranged to suit your team.
The course is assessed by a multiple-choice examination taken under exam conditions, with no books or learning materials allowed. The exam has 40 questions and the pass mark is 28 out of 40.
Yes — if you have a group of people we can come to your venue and deliver the course. You'll need a suitable room to deliver the training in, set up appropriately for conducting the examination. Please contact us to discuss your requirements.
You'll need a room with adequate space to set up the exam properly — enough room for candidates to sit the assessment individually, without being able to see each other's papers. We'll confirm the exact layout requirements with you when your booking is arranged.
You'll need a laptop or desktop computer with a working camera. Tablets and mobile phones are not suitable for the online classroom or the exam that follows, so please make sure you have access to a proper computer before your session.
Your device must be able to download and run the exam software — we'll send setup instructions in advance so you can check this beforehand. You'll also need:
  • A quiet room where you won't be disturbed during the exam
  • A space free from books, notes, or anything else that could be used to help answer exam questions
  • No headphones or earpieces to be worn during the exam
You'll be given the contact details for the remote invigilation company, who will support you throughout. You can also live chat with them during the exam if you run into any issues.
Unfortunately if the exam slot is missed, a rebooking fee of £50 per learner is charged to arrange a new sitting.
Whichever delivery method you choose — onsite, e-learning, or online classroom — you'll need valid photo ID to sit the exam. A passport or photocard driving licence is accepted.
We'll support you with further training until you're ready to re-sit the examination.
We may be able to make reasonable adjustments for you in the exam, such as large print for impaired sight or coloured paper for dyslexia. If you or a member of your team has a learning difficulty or disability, please contact us as soon as possible to discuss in confidence.
Your exam is marked by the trainer at the end of the session, giving you a provisional mark. Exam papers are then subject to verification by our internal quality assurance team and the awarding organisation before the final award is issued. Certificates are typically posted within 10 working days of verification — the average turnaround is currently around one week from the exam.
Our Personal Licence course is accredited through Highfield Qualifications or Qualifications Network UK — both Ofqual-regulated awarding organisations. The course specification is available on request.

Your Personal Licence

You'll need to apply for your Personal Licence through your local Licensing Authority (local council) — the one covering the area where you normally live.
You'll need to send the following to your local Licensing Authority:
  • Completed Personal Licence application form
  • Declaration of criminal offences
  • Basic Disclosure
  • Two passport photos (one endorsed)
  • Application fee
  • Original certificate of qualification — Level 2 Award for Personal Licence Holders
The application fee is payable to your local Licensing Authority and is currently £37. All Personal Licences are now valid indefinitely, unless surrendered or revoked.
Basic Disclosures are obtained through the Disclosure and Barring Service and currently cost £21.50. To apply you'll need all your addresses for the last 5 years and the dates you lived there, your National Insurance number, a debit or credit card, your passport and your driving licence. You can apply online at gov.uk/request-copy-criminal-record.
Similar to a passport application, one photo should be countersigned as a true likeness by a professional person such as a teacher, doctor, or solicitor.
If you have any unspent relevant convictions as detailed in the Licensing Act 2003, your application will be referred to the Chief of Police for your area, who may object to the issue of a licence. If they do, the Licensing Authority will hold a hearing where you can present your case. After the hearing they will either grant the licence or reject it — if rejected, you have the right of appeal to the Magistrates' Court within 21 days.
